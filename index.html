Investment Glossary

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)

A method of investing a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds, allowing you to build wealth over time through the power of compounding.

Mutual Fund

A professionally managed investment vehicle that pools money from multiple investors to invest in a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other securities.

NAV (Net Asset Value)

The market value per share of a mutual fund, calculated by dividing the total value of the fund's assets minus its liabilities by the number of shares outstanding.

Expense Ratio

The annual fee charged by mutual funds for managing your money, expressed as a percentage of the fund's average assets under management.

CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)

The annual rate of return on an investment, assuming the returns are reinvested and compounded over time.
